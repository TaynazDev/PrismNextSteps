Prism Drop

A time capsule app for Gen Z. Lock memories, messages, and moments until a future date. AI writes letters to your future self and reflects on what you predicted when capsules unlock.

CategorySocial / Memory
PlatformsiOS · Web
Retention driverPeople don't delete apps with locked memories inside
Why it works

Undeleted forever

Once a capsule is locked inside Drop, users will never uninstall it. The app literally holds their future. One of the strongest retention mechanics possible.

Viral by nature

Shared capsule unlocks are inherently shareable — "my friend sent me a time capsule that just unlocked" is a story people tell. Built-in organic spread.

Core features

What Drop does

01

Personal capsules

Write a message, record a voice note, upload a photo or video — lock it until any future date. The unlock date cannot be moved earlier. Ever.

02

AI letter writer

Tell AI how you're feeling right now and where you want to be. It writes a personalised letter to your future self. Lock it without reading it first.

03

Shared capsules

Send a capsule to a friend — a birthday message, a bet, a shared memory — that unlocks on a specific date for both of you. Neither can open it early.

04

Group capsules

A friend group all contributes — photos, predictions, messages. Unlocks at graduation, a reunion, or any milestone the group sets together.

05

AI unlock reflection

When a capsule unlocks, AI reads what you wrote, compares to your current Grind progress and Mood history, and generates a then-vs-now reflection.

06

Prism Rewind — New Year event

Every Jan 1, all capsules set for "this year" unlock simultaneously. The whole Prism community experiences it together — massive annual viral moment.

UX principles

What the experience must feel like

The lock is sacred

Users must never unlock early — not even premium users. The entire emotional value of Drop depends on the lock date being absolute. This is a trust product first.

The unlock should feel like an event

Full-screen animation, a build-up, a moment. Think opening a present. The unlock screen is the most important UI in the whole app — spend 80% of design time on it.

Creating should feel meaningful, not mechanical

Guide users conversationally: "What's happening in your life right now?", "What do you hope changes?", "What do you never want to forget?" AI fills the letter from their answers.

Push notifications

The unlock notification is the most important moment in the product. It needs to feel special — custom sound, full-screen push.

Frontend

Share as much code as possible between web and iOS. The lock/unlock animation is the centrepiece interaction — invest heavily.

If someone in a group capsule deletes their Prism account before the unlock date, what do other recipients see? Needs a clear policy before launch.

Prism Grind

An AI goal operating system for Gen Z. Tell it your big dream, it reverse-engineers it into a weekly roadmap. One daily mission, adaptive AI coaching, and social accountability built right in.

CategoryProductivity / AI
PlatformsiOS · Web · Desktop
Key insightOne mission per day — not a list. This is the whole design philosophy.
Why it works

AI that adapts, not judges

Most productivity apps make you feel guilty when you miss a day. Grind recalibrates — it adjusts the plan, not your self-worth. This emotional difference is everything.

One mission, not a list

Always one focused action per day. Not ten tasks. Not a checklist. This single design decision is what makes it feel achievable rather than overwhelming.

Core features

What Grind does

01

Goal intake — plain language

"I want to get into uni", "I want to ship my first app", "I want 10k followers". AI turns any dream into a structured roadmap with phases and weekly checkpoints.

02

Daily mission

One focused task every day. Completing it earns XP. Mood data from Prism Mood adjusts difficulty — stretch missions on high-energy days, lighter ones when you're low.

03

Weekly AI debrief

Every Sunday, AI reviews your week — what you hit, what you missed, patterns it spotted. Then it adjusts the plan. Feels like a personal coach checking in, not a report card.

04

Social accountability

Friends follow your goals and cheer milestones. Hitting a goal posts to Prism social automatically. Others can join the same goal and race to it alongside you.

05

XP + streak system

Gamified progress. Missing a day pauses the streak — it doesn't reset progress. Eliminates the "I missed one day so I quit" spiral that kills most productivity apps.

06

Multi-goal support

Up to 3 goals simultaneously (more with Grind Pro). AI balances daily missions so they don't clash. Each goal has its own roadmap and progress track.

AI system design

How the AI brain works

Goal decomposition — on creation

Send a structured prompt to Claude: the user's goal, their timeframe, current skill level, and daily time available. Claude returns a JSON roadmap with phases, weekly targets, and sample missions. Stored and referenced for the entire goal lifecycle.

Daily mission generation — each morning

Pull the current phase, yesterday's completion status, and today's Mood score. Ask Claude: "Given phase X of this goal, yesterday's result, and today's energy level Y, what is the single best mission for today?" Returns one task with a time estimate.

Weekly recalibration — every Sunday

Send the full week's data: missions assigned, completed, Mood scores, user notes. Claude assesses progress, adjusts the roadmap if needed, and writes a personalised weekly report. Tone: honest but encouraging.

Open questions
?

What if the goal is too vague?

Goals like "be happier" are hard to decompose. Need a goal refinement step — AI asks clarifying questions before generating the roadmap. Build this into onboarding.

?

What if the goal changes mid-way?

Allow editing with AI re-planning, but preserve completed milestones so progress isn't lost psychologically. Show clearly that the roadmap will be regenerated.

?

Can users game mood scores to get easier missions?

If users log 1/5 every day to get lighter Grind missions, the app stops being useful. Consider using a rolling 3-day average rather than today's score alone.

Prism Roast

AI that tells you the truth. Upload your work — portfolio, code, essay, design, social bio — and get a specific, honest critique followed by a concrete upgrade checklist. Funny. Useful. Built to be shared.

CategoryAI Utility / Fun
PlatformsWeb · iOS
Build orderRecommended first — lowest effort, highest viral potential
Why it works

Gen Z wants actual honesty

The generation that grew up on "ratio" and "L + no cap" wants direct feedback — they're tired of empty validation. Roast gives them honesty wrapped in humour.

Shareable by design

"Look at what AI said about my portfolio" is a post people make. Every share is a free ad. No growth hacking needed — virality is baked into the core format.

Core features

What Roast does

01

Multi-format input

Paste a URL, paste code, upload a PDF (essay, CV), upload an image (design, screenshot), or paste a social bio. Roast handles any format.

02

Tone slider

Gentle nudge → Honest friend → Full roast. Users choose how brutal. Same feedback, different delivery. The slider is the centrepiece UI element.

03

The roast

A punchy, specific critique using the actual content. "Your hero section is giving 2019 Bootstrap template" hits differently than "improve your design." Specificity is everything.

04

Upgrade checklist

After the roast: a concrete numbered list of exactly what to fix, prioritised. The roast gets attention. The checklist delivers the actual value.

05

Send to VibesAI

If the roast is about a website or code project, one tap opens it in VibesAI with the roast feedback pre-loaded as the first prompt. Roast to fix in seconds.

06

Share your roast

One-tap share to Prism social as a stylised card — shows your score, the most savage line, and your username. Friends can add their own burns in comments.

AI prompt engineering

Making the roast actually good

The system prompt is everything

Instruct Claude to: reference actual content (not generic advice), match the tone slider precisely, always end with actionable improvements, and never be mean-spirited. The goal is growth, not destruction. Test this prompt more than anything else in the app.

Three distinct tone variants

Three separate system prompts — one per tone. "Gentle" = supportive mentor with kind honesty. "Full roast" = sharp-witted critic who is always constructive. Test all three extensively with real submissions before launch.

Input type routing

URL = fetch page content server-side then analyse. Image = Claude vision describes before critiquing. Code = analyse structure, readability, best practices. Essay = argument, structure, clarity. Each input type needs a tailored prompt prefix.

Suite connections

"Fix this in VibesAI" on code and website roasts. Pre-loads the project and roast feedback as the first prompt. Seamless cross-app handoff.

Grind

Roast a Grind progress report: "Here's my goal and what I've done — roast my progress." Adds honest accountability to the goal loop.

Prism Social

Share roast cards to Prism social. Friends can comment with their own takes. The card format is designed to drive engagement.

Mood

If today's mood score is very low, Roast defaults to "Gentle nudge" tone regardless of slider setting. Don't kick people when they're down.

Paste text or URL, pick tone, get roast + checklist. Web only. Shareable card. Ship this first — lowest effort, highest viral return.

Image + vision roasts

Open questions
?

How to prevent roasting other people's work?

Designed for self-improvement, not bullying. Terms of service must be clear. Consider a "this is my work" checkbox and content moderation on shared cards.

?

Cost ceiling per user

URL scraping + vision + Claude per roast could get expensive. Model cost per roast at each tone level and set free tier limits accordingly before launch.

Prism Mood

An AI emotional intelligence layer. 10-second daily check-in. Over time, AI spots patterns and quietly personalises the entire Prism suite based on how you're feeling. Not therapy — self-knowledge.

CategoryAI Wellness
PlatformsiOS · Web
Suite roleInvisible intelligence layer across all Prism apps
Why it works

10 seconds a day — zero friction

The check-in takes under 10 seconds. A single tap on a mood scale, optional one-word tag. That's it. The magic is in what AI does with that tiny data point over time.

The glue of the Prism suite

Mood is the only Prism app that makes every other app better by existing. It's infrastructure disguised as a wellness app — and that's its superpower.

Core features

What Mood does

01

Daily check-in

1–5 scale tap. Optional one-word context tag: "tired", "hyped", "anxious", "creative". Optional voice note. Under 10 seconds. Every day.

02

Weekly AI insight

Claude analyses the week's data and surfaces patterns: best days, worst days, correlations. "You feel best on Thursdays. Your lowest days follow 3+ hours of screen time."

03

Suite personalisation

Mood score is available to all Prism apps via an internal API. Each app reads today's score and adapts the experience. This is the killer feature — built over time, not at launch.

04

Mood history + heatmap

A calendar heatmap and trend line showing mood over the past month and year. Tap any day to see the tag and what was happening. Your emotional year in data.

05

Close friend sharing

Share your mood with up to 5 close friends — not public. Just your inner circle can see your vibe today. They can send a quick reaction. Intimate, not performative.

06

Crisis-aware design

If a user logs very low moods for 3+ consecutive days, Mood gently surfaces wellbeing resources. Not alarming — warm and human. This is a non-negotiable safety feature.

Shared mood API

How Mood feeds the rest of Prism

The mood/today endpoint

Mood exposes an internal API: GET /api/mood/today?user_id=X — returns today's score (1–5), tag, and a derived energy level (low / medium / high). Every Prism app calls this on load and adapts accordingly. User data stays in their account — zero third-party access.

What each app does with the score

Grind: Score 1–2 = lighter mission, no streak pressure. Score 4–5 = stretch mission offered.

Roast: Score 1–2 = tone defaults to Gentle nudge regardless of slider position.

Drop: Score 4–5 = suggest creating a memory capsule. "Today feels special — lock it."

Prism Social: Score 1–2 = feed slows down, shows calmer content, hides high-stimulation posts.

VibesAI: Score 1–2 = suggest calmer project types. Score 4–5 = encourage ambitious builds.

Notifications

Privacy

Mood data never leaves Prism servers. No analytics, no ads, no training on user mood data. State this explicitly in onboarding.

Design principles

This app must feel safe

Never make users feel judged

Low mood scores should never trigger guilt or unsolicited advice. The UI response to a 1/5 should feel like a warm hug, not a health alert. Colour, copy, and micro-interactions all need to reinforce this.

Privacy by default

Nothing is shared unless the user explicitly enables it. State clearly in onboarding: "Your mood data stays on your account. We never share or sell it."

Crisis handling — required before launch

3+ consecutive days of score 1 = gently surface wellbeing resources. This feature must be reviewed by someone with mental health expertise before launch. Calm, warm, human — never alarming. This is a non-negotiable safety requirement.
